Project Description

This semester we will be producing systems that can respond to the environment using serial sensing and actuation in your system for a mobile weather station. Each team will also broadcast their environmental data to the internet over WiFi using the MQTT protocol.

Our team was tasked with developing a mobile weather station that would be capable of measuring at least two different environmental variables, including but not limited to:

  • Temperature
  • Rainfall
  • Humidity
  • Atmospheric pressure
  • Windspeed

In addition to monitoring the eviorment, we were required to utilize an actuator who's movement would be based on data collected by our sensors.

Developing User Needs and Requirments

With the project requirements in mind, we developed user needs and requirements for our product based on user reviews.

User Needs

Steps Involved in our User Needs Generation:

  1. Researched similar products on the market and analyzed user reviews.
  2. Sorted user needs into categories:
    1. Data based
    2. Location based
    3. General
  3. Ranked User Needs

Unexpected User Needs:

  • Battery lubricant
  • Fan aspirated temperature sensor

Product Requirments

Steps Involved in our Product Requirments Generation:

  1. Identify Stakeholders
    • Park Rangers/ National Park Associations
    • Camping/ Hiking Clubs
    • Petroleum Companies
    • Farmers
  2. Brainstormed possible Use Cases based on identified stakeholders (Shown above)
  3. Brainstormed Product Requirements based on User Needs
    • The product must require no prior training to deploy
    • Display “Feels Like” Temperatures
    • The device must be transportable by an average human
  4. Rank generated product requirments

Component Selection

Market research and selection of the optimal component to fit our design requirements.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Switching Voltage Regulator Switching Voltage Regulator

LM2673SX-ADJ/NOPB
  • Input voltage of 8V to 40V
  • 3.3, 5, and 12V fixed voltage output
  • 1.2-37V adjustable output
  • Simple design with passive components
  • Requires Diode
  • Large package, 10x5mm

The LM2673SX-ADJ/NOPB is similar to the LM2575T-3.3G in the sense of similar configuration. It is much cheaper than the other options, and it is a good size that should provide easy soldering. Additionally, the output voltage and current of the LM2673SX-ADJ/NOPB are adjustable through the use of resistors.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Temperature Sensor Temperature Sensor

AT30TS74-XM8M-T
  • Low operating current of 85 microA
  • +/- 1 degree Celsius accuracy of temperatures between -20 to 100 degrees Celsius
  • 1.2-37V adjustable output
  • 9-12 bit configurable resolution
  • 8 MSOP, easier hand soldering
  • Cheap
  • Takes up more space than other options

Initially the TC74A4-3.3VCTTR seemed ideal since we would be working with that component in class, and it is a digital temperature sensor with I2C communication. The AT30TS74-XM8M-T is very similar to the TC74A4-3.3VCTTR, but it has a bit larger package which should be easier to hand solder. Additionally, it has a lower operating current, more accurate, and is more of a configurable resolution.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Power Battery

PS-1230 F1
  • Robust
  • Rechargeable
  • Great capacity
  • Weather resistant
  • Long lifetime
  • Expensive
  • Large
  • Heavy

The PS-1230 F1 is energy dense and widely available. It is also more resistant to weather effects compared to lithium based batteries and isn't nearly as volatile. It is an easy pick due to these factors, giving us ample room in our power budget. In addition to giving us tons of power, the fact that it’s a nickel-lead acid battery means that finding supporting components (like battery clips, chargers, ect.) are cheap and easy to find.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Motor Motor

PPN13PB11C2
  • Cheap
  • Easy to acquire
  • Simple
  • Small
  • Decent power
  • Can run at 6V
  • Doesn't include any gears

The PPN13PB11C2 motor is cheap, small, and widely available, allowing us to easily integrate it into our design. It may not be the most powerful motor listed, but it should be able to give enough performance for our purposes. Lastly, it's also not the most power efficient, but it's not a power hog and shouldn’t require much from the batteries/solar panels.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Motor Motor Driver 1

IFX9201SGAUMA1
  • Small
  • Cheap
  • Already known
  • SPI communication
  • Meets peralta manufacturing requirements

Motor Motor Driver 2

DRV8830DGQR
  • Small
  • Cheap
  • I2C communication
  • Variable control PWM
  • Can only supply 5V at max
  • Does not meet peralta manufacturing requirements

Our rationale for choosing IFX9201SGAUMA1 is that it provides a robust and cheap solution. Although it may only supply 5.5V, this is somewhat bypassed by the fact that it can supply a max of 10 amps. We also choose DRV8830DGQR as it uses I2C to communicate, allowing us to control the motor speed, however, it has a very small footprint and thus will be hard to work with

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Light Sensor Light Sensor

NSL-5152
  • Easy to use
  • Extensive documentation and use cases
  • Cheap
  • Small
  • Low response time
  • Through hole mounting

The NSL-5152 has the most example cases geared towards tracking the position of the sun. It also doesn’t require the use of an external active IC like the TEMD7000X01. Due to the nature of how the team is using the light sensor, a through-hole mount would actually be more beneficial since the light sensor would have to be mounted next to the moving solar panels separate from the main PCB.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Analog to Digital ADC

MCP3021A5T-E/OT
  • Cheap
  • Surface mount
  • Detailed I2C documentation
  • Meets peralta manufacturing requirements
  • Low resolution (10-bit)

The MCP3021A5T has the most versatility of the three chips along with an extensive datasheet. Unlike the 5591, this chip fits within the project's requirements and is far cheaper. It also has an optimal pad size which will allow for the reliable manufacture of prototypes.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
“Anemometer” Hall Sensor Hall Sensor

AS5600-ASOM
  • Simple I2C protocol
  • High-resolution output signal
  • Selectable output
  • Low-power consumption
  • Easy to setup
  • Small
  • Robust environmental tolerance
  • Takes up more space than other options

Our rationale for choosing the AS5600-ASOM is that we are familiar with the chip. We have used it in class, and there is an ample supply already in class. Since the differences between the chips are minor, familiarity is the deciding factor for us.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Solar Solar Charger

AG103
  • Charges Lead Nickel acid battery
  • Outputs highly stable and regulated voltage
  • Overcurrent protection
  • Short protection
  • Can output 3A max
  • Mid-range price
  • Outputs 12V
  • Needs at least an input voltage of 9V

Our rationale for choosing the AG103 is that it is versatile and robust. By combining a solar charger and a battery protection board, we assure that our batteries will be fed consistent power and be protected.

Subsystem Component Name Pros Cons
Solar Solar Panels

Voltaic Systems P123
  • Small
  • Cheap
  • Can be series up to 18V
  • Max power of 0.1 amps

Our rationale for choosing the Voltaic Systems P123 is that it provides a robust and cheap solution. Although it may only supply 0.1 amps, this can be mitigated by adding more solar panels.
